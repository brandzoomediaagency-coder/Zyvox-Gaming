How to choose a gaming keyboard in India (2026 buying guide)

Mechanical or membrane?

Mechanical switches give a consistent, tactile and durable press most gamers prefer. Good mech-dome boards like the G213 are still comfortable and cheaper — a fine starting point on a tight budget.

Which switch: blue, brown or red?

Red switches are smooth and linear (best for fast gaming), brown are tactile but quiet, and blue are clicky and loud. For gaming most players choose red or brown.

TKL, 60% or full-size?

TKL drops the numpad for more mouse room and is the most popular gaming size. 60% is ultra-compact for low-sensitivity players; full-size keeps the numpad if you need it for work.

Do I need hot-swap or wireless?

Hot-swap lets you change switches without soldering — nice for tinkerers. Wireless (like the Keychron K2) keeps your desk tidy and can still run wired over USB-C for zero latency.

Is a mechanical keyboard better for gaming?▾

Generally yes — mechanical switches are more consistent, tactile and durable. Good mech-dome boards like the Logitech G213 remain fine budget options.

What's the difference between blue, brown and red switches?▾

Blue are clicky/loud, brown are tactile/quieter, and red are smooth linear switches favoured for fast gaming. Pick red or brown for gaming.

Is a TKL or 60% keyboard better for gaming?▾

TKL keeps arrow keys and is the most popular gaming size; 60% is more compact for maximum mouse room but moves some keys to secondary layers.
