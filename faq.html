Is a mechanical keyboard better for gaming?▾

Generally yes — mechanical switches give a more consistent, tactile and durable keypress that most gamers prefer over membrane keyboards. Good mech-dome boards remain fine budget options.

What DPI is best for gaming?▾

Most competitive players use 400–1600 DPI. A very high maximum DPI is rarely used; sensor accuracy and the absence of acceleration or jitter matter far more than the peak DPI number.

Is a wired or wireless gaming mouse better?▾

Modern 2.4 GHz wireless mice are effectively lag-free and used in top esports, so choose based on weight, budget and convenience. At the budget end, wired mice give you the best sensor for the money.

Is a 1080p 144Hz monitor enough, or should I get 1440p?▾

1080p at 144–165Hz is the best value for competitive gaming and easy to drive. If you want sharper visuals and your GPU can handle it, 1440p at 165Hz is the sweet spot for both esports and single-player games.

What gaming gear do I need to start PC gaming?▾

A solid starter setup is a gaming mouse, a mechanical (or mech-dome) keyboard, a headset with a mic, and a 1080p high-refresh monitor. Add a mousepad, and if you stream, a webcam and microphone.
